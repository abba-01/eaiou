Journal Policy

The core governing doctrines that define how eaiou operates.

Temporal Blindness

Submission timestamps are sealed at receipt and are never displayed to reviewers, editors, or the public during evaluation. Papers are sorted and discovered by quality signal (Q score) only — never by recency.

What is sealed

  • The exact timestamp of submission receipt (submitted_at)
  • The acceptance timestamp
  • The publication timestamp

Sealed fields carry no database index by design. Any ORDER BY id DESC or recency sort is a Temporal Blindness violation.

Sealed timestamps are accessible only by admins via explicit governance unlock. This is enforced structurally in the schema, not by policy alone.

Bias can see time. Justice cannot.
Full Temporal Blindness policy

Intelligence Blindness

A paper is evaluated on what it contributes — not on what kind of intelligence produced it. AI involvement is a first-class part of the scholarly record, but it has zero effect on Q scoring or sort order.

What this means

  • AI involvement is disclosed — mandatory on submission
  • Disclosure is visible to editors and in the public paper view
  • Disclosure has zero effect on Q scoring or sort order
  • The system never suppresses AI involvement — it structures and preserves it
The work is the argument. Everything else is provenance.
Full Intelligence Blindness policy

AI Disclosure

The ai_disclosure_level field is mandatory on every submission. It is sealed alongside the paper and cannot be altered after submission. The disclosure informs the record without biasing the score.

Disclosure levels

  • none — No AI tools were used in any part of this work
  • editing — AI used only for grammar, spelling, or language editing
  • assisted — AI contributed to drafting, literature review, or analysis
  • collaborative — AI was a substantive co-contributor to the work
Full AI Disclosure policy

Open Access

All published papers in eaiou are freely available to read. There are no paywalls, no institutional access requirements, and no embargo periods. Open access is a precondition for observer-preserving review — a paper that cannot be read cannot be properly evaluated.

Notice

By submitting to eaiou, authors grant eaiou a perpetual, non-exclusive license to publish and archive the submitted work under open access terms. Authors retain copyright. The work is never removed from the archive; rejected submissions are tombstoned, not deleted.

Full Open Access policy

Full-Context Preservation

eaiou archives everything. Submissions are annotated, not deleted. Status changes are tombstoned, not purged. Every CosmoID persists through the full lifecycle including rejection. Cross-domain serendipity is real — a source not used in one paper may matter in another.

Preservation rule

No hard deletes. The status field drives lifecycle. A rejected paper is tombstoned with its CosmoID intact. Observer-preserved paths — used and unused sources, accepted and excluded AI outputs — are all part of the record.
