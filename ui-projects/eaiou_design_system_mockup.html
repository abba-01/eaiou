Hubble Tension as Measurement Artifact: Evidence from Spatial Encoding Constraints in CMB Pipelines

◈ Transparency: 6 Sources · 2 Datasets ⬡ AI-Logged: Claude Sonnet, GPT-4.1 rs:NullResult rs:CrossDomain
q_signal 9.24

We present a framework in which the observed Hubble tension — the disagreement between early-universe and late-universe measurements of H₀ — arises not from new physics but from systematic bias introduced by the spatial encoding assumptions embedded in CMB-based measurement pipelines.

The key insight is that standard CMB analysis assumes a fixed spatial encoding geometry inherited from ΛCDM. When this geometry is relaxed and observer-relative coordinate frames are permitted, the tension between Planck-derived H₀ values and direct distance-ladder measurements dissolves within statistical uncertainties.
