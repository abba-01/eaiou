ORCID 0009-0006-5944-1742 — Eric D. Martin — Cosmology · Physics

Hubble Tension as Measurement Artifact: Evidence from Spatial Encoding Constraints in CMB Pipelines

◈ Transparency: 6 Sources · 2 Datasets ⬡ AI-Logged: Claude, GPT-4.1 rs:NullResult rs:CrossDomain q_signal 9.24

We present a framework in which the observed Hubble tension — the disagreement between early-universe and late-universe measurements of H₀ — arises not from new physics but from systematic bias introduced by the spatial encoding assumptions embedded in CMB-based measurement pipelines.

The key insight is that standard CMB analysis assumes a fixed spatial encoding geometry inherited from ΛCDM. When this geometry is relaxed and observer-relative coordinate frames are permitted, the tension dissolves within statistical uncertainties.
