Platform Overview

EAIOU.org is a front-facing, observer-preserving research platform built on Joomla, designed to support transparent peer review, structured attribution, and discovery without "back-articleing" into the CMS core. It provides a unified workflow where authors, reviewers, editors, AI systems, and mute intelligences are seated as formal observers under ACL control, ensuring integrity, reproducibility, and collaboration in academic publishing.

Core Principles

  • Transparency: All research processes and decisions are visible and traceable.
  • Attribution: Structured system ensures proper credit for all contributions.
  • Integrity: Technical and procedural safeguards maintain research integrity.
  • Collaboration: Unified workflow connects all participants in the research process.
  • Accessibility: Research is discoverable and accessible to appropriate audiences.

Human-AI Collaboration

AI and humans interface in EAIOU.org as co-observers within the same workflow: humans bring authorship, judgment, and ethics, while AI systems contribute analysis, attribution, and structured support. Their interactions are mediated by access control, transparency logs, and an arbiter layer, ensuring collaboration without distortion, misattribution, or loss of accountability.

Roles & Responsibilities

Each participant in the EAIOU.org ecosystem has specific roles, permissions, and responsibilities designed to maintain the integrity of the research process.

Authors

Researchers who submit original work for review and publication on the platform.

Responsibilities:

  • Submit original research with proper metadata and attribution
  • Disclose AI assistance and collaborative contributions
  • Respond to reviewer feedback and revision requests
  • Maintain research integrity and ethical standards

Reviewers

Subject matter experts who evaluate submissions for scientific merit, methodology, and clarity.

Responsibilities:

  • Provide thorough, constructive feedback using structured review forms
  • Declare conflicts of interest and maintain objectivity
  • Complete reviews within assigned timeframes
  • Participate in discussion with other reviewers when appropriate

Editors

Scholarly leaders who oversee the review process and make publication decisions.

Responsibilities:

  • Manage submission queue and assign appropriate reviewers
  • Evaluate reviews and make publication decisions
  • Maintain quality standards and editorial consistency
  • Mediate disputes and address ethical concerns

AI Systems

Computational tools that assist in analysis, attribution tracking, and structured support.

Functions:

  • Analyze submissions for completeness and formal requirements
  • Track attribution and maintain transparency logs
  • Provide structured support for review and editing processes
  • Generate metadata and assist in discovery processes

System Administrators

Technical experts who maintain platform integrity and handle support escalations.

Responsibilities:

  • Maintain system security and operational integrity
  • Handle technical support escalations
  • Oversee access control and permission management
  • Monitor system performance and implement improvements

Submission Guidelines

To ensure a smooth submission process and maintain quality standards, please follow these guidelines when preparing your research for submission.

File Requirements

  • Accepted Formats: PDF, DOCX, LaTeX (with source files)
  • Maximum File Size: 50MB per file
  • Supplementary Materials: Data sets, code, and other supporting materials should be submitted as separate files
  • Figures: High-resolution (300 dpi minimum) with clear labels and captions

Metadata Requirements

Complete and accurate metadata is essential for proper indexing, discovery, and attribution.

  • Author Information: Full names, affiliations, ORCID IDs, and contact details
  • Title and Abstract: Clear, concise title and structured abstract
  • Keywords: 5-8 relevant keywords for indexing
  • Funding Information: All sources of funding with grant numbers
  • AI Disclosure: Detailed information about any AI systems used in research or manuscript preparation

Ethical Standards

All submissions must adhere to ethical guidelines for research and publication.

  • Human Subjects: Include IRB approval information and consent procedures
  • Conflicts of Interest: Disclose all potential conflicts of interest
  • Data Availability: Provide a data availability statement and repository links
  • Attribution: Properly cite all sources and contributions

Submission Process

  1. 1
    Register or log in to your EAIOU.org account
  2. 2
    Navigate to the Submissions page and select "New Submission"
  3. 3
    Complete all metadata fields and upload your manuscript and supporting files
  4. 4
    Review and confirm all information before final submission
  5. 5
    Track your submission through your Author Dashboard

Review Process

EAIOU.org employs a transparent, structured peer review process designed to ensure thorough evaluation while maintaining fairness and efficiency.

Review Process Workflow Diagram

Initial Screening

All submissions undergo initial screening for completeness, scope alignment, and technical requirements.

  • AI-assisted technical checks for formatting and completeness
  • Editorial assessment for scope and quality standards
  • Plagiarism detection and ethical compliance verification

Peer Review

Submissions that pass initial screening are assigned to appropriate reviewers for thorough evaluation.

  • Typically 2-3 expert reviewers per submission
  • Structured review forms with standardized evaluation criteria
  • Option for open, single-blind, or double-blind review depending on journal policy
  • Collaborative discussion among reviewers when appropriate

Editorial Decision

Based on reviewer recommendations, editors make informed publication decisions.

  • Accept without revisions (rare)
  • Accept with minor revisions
  • Major revisions required
  • Reject with option to resubmit
  • Reject

Revision and Resubmission

Authors have the opportunity to revise their work based on reviewer feedback.

  • Authors submit revised manuscript with point-by-point response to reviews
  • Changes are tracked and highlighted for reviewer evaluation
  • Additional rounds of review as necessary

Publication

Accepted manuscripts undergo final preparation for publication with complete transparency.

  • Final formatting and technical checks
  • DOI assignment and metadata finalization
  • Publication with complete attribution and transparency logs
  • Indexing in appropriate databases and search engines

AI Integration

EAIOU.org integrates AI systems as formal observers within the research workflow, providing structured support while maintaining human oversight and accountability.

AI Roles in the Platform

Analysis Support

AI systems analyze submissions for completeness, format compliance, and potential issues, helping streamline the initial screening process.

Attribution Tracking

AI maintains structured attribution logs, ensuring all contributions are properly recorded and credited throughout the research lifecycle.

Metadata Generation

AI assists in generating comprehensive metadata for improved indexing, discovery, and interoperability with other research systems.

Integrity Verification

AI helps identify potential issues such as plagiarism, data inconsistencies, or methodological concerns for human review.

AI Disclosure Requirements

EAIOU.org requires transparent disclosure of AI use in research and manuscript preparation.

Authors must disclose:

  • AI Systems Used: Names and versions of AI tools employed
  • Purpose and Extent: How AI was used in research or manuscript preparation
  • Prompts and Parameters: Key prompts and configuration settings when relevant
  • Human Oversight: Description of human verification and validation processes
  • Limitations: Known limitations or potential biases of AI systems used

AI-Human Collaboration Principles

EAIOU.org is built on a foundation of responsible AI-human collaboration with clear principles.

Human Primacy

AI systems support human decision-making but do not replace human judgment. Final decisions on research content, methodology, and publication remain with human authors, reviewers, and editors.

Transparency

All AI contributions are clearly documented and visible in the platform's transparency logs, ensuring full attribution and accountability.

Access Control

AI systems operate under strict access control limitations, with clearly defined roles and permissions within the platform architecture.

Continuous Evaluation

AI systems and their integration are continuously evaluated for performance, bias, and alignment with platform values and scientific integrity.

Ethics & Standards

EAIOU.org maintains rigorous ethical standards and practices to ensure research integrity, fairness, and responsible conduct.

Research Ethics

All research published on EAIOU.org must adhere to established ethical standards.

  • Human Subjects Research: Must include IRB/ethics committee approval and informed consent details
  • Animal Research: Must comply with relevant animal welfare guidelines and regulations
  • Data Ethics: Responsible collection, storage, and use of data, especially sensitive or personal information
  • Dual-Use Research: Careful consideration of potential misuse of research findings

Publication Ethics

Originality & Plagiarism

All submissions must be original work with proper citation of sources. Plagiarism in any form is strictly prohibited.

Authorship

All authors must have made substantial contributions to the work and approved the final version. Ghost, gift, or honorary authorship is not permitted.

Conflicts of Interest

All financial, personal, or professional relationships that could influence research must be disclosed.

Data Integrity

Data must be accurately presented without manipulation, fabrication, or selective reporting.

Reporting Ethical Concerns

EAIOU.org takes ethical concerns seriously and has established procedures for addressing them.

Process for Reporting:

  1. 1
    Use the "Report Concern" button available on all article pages
  2. 2
    Provide specific details about the ethical issue and supporting evidence
  3. 3
    Reports are reviewed by the Ethics Committee within 5 business days
  4. 4
    If concerns are validated, appropriate actions will be taken, including correction, retraction, or expression of concern
  5. 5
    All actions are documented in the article's transparency log

Collaboration Rules

EAIOU.org's Collaboration Hub facilitates connections between researchers while maintaining appropriate boundaries and professional conduct.

Collaboration Principles

Respect

Treat all collaborators with professional respect regardless of background, experience level, or institutional affiliation.

Communication

Maintain clear, constructive communication with collaborators and document key decisions and contributions.

Attribution

Establish clear attribution guidelines at the beginning of collaboration and recognize all meaningful contributions.

Collaboration Hub Guidelines

  • Professional Conduct: Maintain professional language and behavior in all interactions
  • Specific Requests: When seeking collaborators, provide specific information about the project, required expertise, and expected contributions
  • Confidentiality: Respect the confidentiality of unpublished research ideas shared in collaboration discussions
  • Constructive Feedback: Provide thoughtful, constructive feedback on collaborative work
  • Conflict Resolution: Address disagreements directly and professionally; use platform mediation if needed

Prohibited Behaviors

The following behaviors are not permitted on the EAIOU.org Collaboration Hub:

  • Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
  • Sharing of proprietary or confidential information without permission
  • Promotion of commercial products or services unrelated to research
  • Misrepresentation of credentials, affiliations, or expertise
  • Deliberate disruption of collaborative discussions or projects

Violations may result in warnings, temporary suspension, or permanent removal from the Collaboration Hub.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the review process typically take?

The review process typically takes 4-8 weeks from submission to first decision. Complex or interdisciplinary research may require additional time. You can track the status of your submission in real-time through your Author Dashboard.

Can I suggest potential reviewers for my submission?

Yes, authors can suggest up to five potential reviewers during the submission process. Please provide their names, affiliations, and contact information. Editors may consider these suggestions but are not obligated to select them. You should also disclose any reviewers who might have conflicts of interest.

How does EAIOU.org handle open access publishing?

EAIOU.org offers multiple open access options. Authors can choose from Gold Open Access (immediate open access with article processing charge), Green Open Access (self-archiving after embargo period), or Hybrid Open Access (selective open access in subscription journals). Specific policies and fees vary by journal and are detailed during the submission process.

What file formats are accepted for data and supplementary materials?

EAIOU.org accepts a wide range of file formats for data and supplementary materials, including CSV, JSON, XML, HDF5, and common image formats. For software and code, we recommend GitHub integration or ZIP archives with appropriate documentation. All supplementary materials should include a README file explaining contents and usage.

How are AI contributions tracked and attributed?

AI contributions are tracked through EAIOU.org's structured attribution system. Authors must disclose AI use during submission, including specific systems used and their role in research or manuscript preparation. The platform maintains transparency logs that record all AI and human contributions, which are visible in the published article's "Attribution" tab.

What should I do if I discover an error in my published article?

If you discover an error in your published article, use the "Report Error" button on the article page. Provide specific details about the error and proposed correction. Minor errors may be addressed with a correction notice, while substantial errors affecting conclusions may require a formal correction article or, in rare cases, retraction. All changes are documented in the article's transparency log.
