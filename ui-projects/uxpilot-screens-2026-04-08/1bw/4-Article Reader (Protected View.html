Observer-Preserving Frameworks for Multi-Modal Research Attribution

A. Rodriguez, B. Williams, C. Taylor + AI co-observer

Abstract

This paper introduces a novel framework for preserving observer integrity in multi-modal research environments where human researchers collaborate with artificial intelligence systems. We propose a structured attribution model that maintains clear delineation of contributions while supporting transparent peer review processes.

Through a series of case studies, we demonstrate how access control lists (ACLs) can be implemented to ensure that all observers—human authors, reviewers, editors, AI systems, and mute intelligences—maintain appropriate boundaries while maximizing collaborative potential. Our findings suggest that observer-preserving frameworks significantly improve reproducibility and attribution accuracy in complex research workflows.

The proposed framework addresses key challenges in modern academic publishing, including misattribution, distortion of contributions, and accountability gaps. Implementation recommendations for academic platforms are provided, with specific attention to integration with existing content management systems.
