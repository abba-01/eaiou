Platform Overview

EAIOU.org is a front-facing, observer-preserving research platform built on Joomla, designed to support transparent peer review, structured attribution, and discovery without "back-articleing" into the CMS core. It provides a unified workflow where authors, reviewers, editors, AI systems, and mute intelligences are seated as formal observers under ACL control, ensuring integrity, reproducibility, and collaboration in academic publishing.

AI and humans interface in EAIOU.org as co-observers within the same workflow: humans bring authorship, judgment, and ethics, while AI systems contribute analysis, attribution, and structured support. Their interactions are mediated by access control, transparency logs, and an arbiter layer, ensuring collaboration without distortion, misattribution, or loss of accountability.

Key Features

  • Structured attribution for all contributors

Observer Roles & Responsibilities

EAIOU.org recognizes multiple observer types, each with specific roles, responsibilities, and access controls. Understanding these roles is essential for all platform users.

Authors

Authors are responsible for the original research content, methodology, and conclusions. They maintain primary intellectual ownership while collaborating with other observers.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Upload original research with complete metadata
  • Disclose AI assistance and contributions accurately
  • Respond to reviewer feedback and revision requests
  • Ensure reproducibility of research methods and results

Reviewers

Reviewers evaluate research for methodological soundness, originality, and contribution to the field. They provide structured feedback within the platform's transparency framework.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Provide objective evaluation using standardized rubrics
  • Assess methodology, results, and conclusions
  • Identify potential issues with reproducibility
  • Participate in structured discussion with authors when appropriate

Editors

Editors oversee the review process, make publication decisions, and ensure adherence to platform guidelines. They act as mediators between authors and reviewers.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Manage submission queue and assign appropriate reviewers
  • Make publication decisions based on reviewer feedback
  • Ensure compliance with platform policies and ethical standards
  • Mediate disputes between authors and reviewers when necessary

AI Systems

AI systems serve as co-observers, providing analysis, attribution support, and structured contributions. Their role is transparent and fully attributed within the platform.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Assist with structured analysis and pattern recognition
  • Support attribution tracking and verification
  • Provide standardized review support within defined parameters
  • Generate structured metadata and research gap analysis

Review Process

Standard Review

  • Reviewer accesses manuscript through secure platform
  • Completes standardized evaluation rubric
  • Provides structured comments and recommendations
  • Editor synthesizes reviews and makes decision

AI-Assisted Review

  • AI performs initial analysis of methodology and structure
  • Human reviewer validates AI findings and adds expertise
  • Dual attribution for all review components
  • Transparency logs record all interactions

Transparency & Attribution

EAIOU.org implements comprehensive transparency and attribution mechanisms to ensure all contributions are properly recorded and credited.

Transparency Logs

All platform interactions are recorded in immutable transparency logs, providing a complete audit trail of:

  • Document versions and changes
  • Review activities and decisions
  • AI system interactions and contributions
  • Editorial decisions and rationales

Attribution Framework

The platform implements a structured attribution framework that:

  • Distinguishes between human and AI contributions
  • Provides granular attribution at the section level
  • Records contribution types (writing, analysis, review)
  • Generates standardized attribution statements

Frequently Asked Questions

EAIOU.org ensures research integrity through multiple mechanisms: transparent peer review processes, comprehensive attribution frameworks, immutable transparency logs, and access control systems that clearly define observer roles and permissions. All platform interactions are recorded and available for audit, creating accountability at every stage of the research lifecycle.

"Observer-preserving" refers to the platform's fundamental approach of maintaining the integrity and identity of each observer (human or AI) throughout the research process. This means that contributions are never anonymized or blended in ways that obscure their source, and each observer's role, permissions, and contributions are clearly defined and recorded.

AI contributions are attributed through our structured attribution framework, which identifies the specific AI system used, the nature of its contributions (analysis, writing assistance, etc.), and the extent of those contributions. AI systems are treated as formal co-observers, with their inputs clearly delineated from human contributions in all published materials and transparency logs.

The arbiter layer is a platform component that mediates interactions between human and AI observers. It enforces access controls, records all interactions in transparency logs, and ensures that contributions maintain their attribution throughout the research process. This layer prevents unauthorized modifications and helps maintain the integrity of the observer-preserving approach.

"Mute intelligences" refers to AI systems that provide analytical support without generating natural language output. These systems might perform data analysis, pattern recognition, or verification tasks, with their contributions recorded in structured formats rather than prose. Like other observer types, their contributions are fully attributed in the platform's transparency framework.
