Ethical Frameworks for Quantum Computing

Created by Admin User on June 15, 2025

Abstract

This article explores the ethical frameworks necessary for governing quantum computing technologies as they become more accessible and powerful. We examine existing ethical guidelines from various disciplines and propose adaptations for the unique challenges posed by quantum computing.

Introduction

Quantum computing represents a paradigm shift in computational capabilities, offering the potential to solve problems currently intractable with classical computers. As these technologies advance, they raise novel ethical considerations that extend beyond those addressed by existing frameworks for digital technologies.

Methodology

This study employs a comparative analysis of ethical frameworks from computer science, bioethics, and emerging technology governance. We conducted interviews with 24 experts in quantum computing, ethics, and policy to identify key concerns specific to quantum technologies.

Results

Our analysis identified four primary ethical domains requiring specialized consideration in quantum computing: cryptographic vulnerability, algorithmic accountability, computational resource distribution, and interdisciplinary research ethics...
