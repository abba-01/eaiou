Editorial Decision Criteria

This guide outlines standardized thresholds and criteria for making editorial decisions based on reviewer feedback. The decision matrix helps maintain consistency and transparency in the peer review process.

Handling Reviewer Disagreement

When Reviewers Disagree

  • Minor disagreement (≤ 15% score variance): Use weighted average of scores with editor discretion.
  • Significant disagreement (16-30% score variance): Carefully evaluate reviewer comments for specific concerns and consider your own assessment of those issues.
  • Major disagreement (> 30% score variance): Consider seeking an additional reviewer or making a direct assessment yourself.

Special Cases

AI-Assisted Content

For articles with AI-assistance declaration, apply additional scrutiny to originality and methodology sections. Consider whether AI tools were properly disclosed and appropriately used.

Speculative Research

For speculative research, evaluate theoretical soundness and potential impact more heavily than empirical validation. Ensure limitations are clearly acknowledged.

Ethical Concerns

If any reviewer raises ethical concerns, escalate to the ethics committee regardless of overall score. This supersedes normal decision thresholds.

Conflicts of Interest

When conflicts of interest are declared, ensure additional independent review. Exclude conflicted reviewers from decision calculations.
