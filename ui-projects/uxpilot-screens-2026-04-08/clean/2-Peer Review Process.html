Peer Review Process

1

Initial Assessment

Review the manuscript's methodology, data quality, and relevance to determine if it meets the basic criteria for publication.

  • Assess methodological soundness
  • Evaluate data quality and analysis
  • Check relevance to the field
2

Detailed Evaluation

Conduct a thorough analysis of the manuscript, examining its scientific merit, originality, and potential impact.

  • Evaluate scientific accuracy
  • Assess originality and innovation
  • Consider potential impact on the field
3

Feedback Submission

Provide constructive feedback and recommendations for improvement, along with your publication decision.

  • Offer constructive criticism
  • Suggest specific improvements
  • Make a recommendation decision

Review Form Example

Article Information

Title

Neural Correlates of Consciousness in Coma Patients

Authors

Dr. Sarah Chen, Dr. Michael Rodriguez, et al.

Journal

Neuroscience Advances

Manuscript ID

NA-2023-0157

Evaluation Criteria

Originality and Significance

4/5 - Very Good

The study presents a novel approach to detecting consciousness in coma patients using advanced neuroimaging techniques. While the methodology is innovative, similar approaches have been explored in recent literature.

Methodology and Data Analysis

3/5 - Good

The methodology is generally sound, but there are some concerns regarding the statistical analysis of the neuroimaging data. The sample size is relatively small (n=24), which limits the generalizability of the findings.

Clarity and Presentation

5/5 - Excellent

The manuscript is exceptionally well-written and organized. The figures are clear and effectively illustrate the key findings. The discussion section provides a comprehensive interpretation of the results in the context of existing literature.

Recommendations

Accept with Minor Revisions

The manuscript presents valuable findings that contribute to our understanding of consciousness in coma patients. However, several issues need to be addressed before publication:

  1. Expand the sample size or provide a power analysis to justify the current sample.
  2. Revise the statistical analysis section to address the concerns regarding multiple comparisons.
  3. Include a more detailed discussion of the limitations of the study.
  4. Consider adding a supplementary analysis of individual patient data to strengthen the findings.

Reviewer Guidelines

Best Practices

  • Provide specific, constructive feedback that helps authors improve their work
  • Assess methodological rigor and statistical validity objectively
  • Evaluate the manuscript based on its scientific merit, not personal preferences
  • Complete reviews within the requested timeframe
  • Declare any potential conflicts of interest
  • Maintain confidentiality of the manuscript and review process

Practices to Avoid

  • Using dismissive or hostile language in your review
  • Requesting unnecessary additional experiments that would significantly delay publication
  • Focusing exclusively on grammar and formatting issues
  • Suggesting citations primarily to increase your own citation count
  • Delaying reviews without justification
  • Sharing manuscript contents with colleagues or using the data for your own research
